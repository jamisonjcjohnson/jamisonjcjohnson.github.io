Amazon Kindle devices are primarily associated with books purchased from the Kindle Store, but they can also display personal eBooks, PDFs, manuals, reports, research papers, and other documents.

Amazon provides several ways to transfer files to a Kindle. The easiest option for most users is the browser-based **Send to Kindle** service because it converts supported files, stores them in the Kindle library, and makes them available across compatible Kindle devices and applications.

Files can also be transferred through email, the Kindle mobile application, the Send to Kindle desktop application, or a direct USB connection.

Only transfer books and documents that you created, purchased without restrictive digital rights management, received legally, or otherwise have permission to use.

In this blog post, we will explain how to transfer books and PDFs to Kindle using Send to Kindle, email, mobile sharing, desktop applications, and USB.

## Transfer Methods at a Glance

The primary methods for transferring personal documents to a Kindle include:

- Send to Kindle through a web browser
- Send to Kindle through email
- Send to Kindle from an iPhone or iPad
- Send to Kindle from an Android device
- Send to Kindle from Windows or macOS
- Manual transfer through a USB cable

For most users, the browser-based Send to Kindle service is the recommended option.

It provides several advantages:

- Files can be added to your Kindle library.
- Documents can be synchronized across compatible devices.
- EPUB files are converted into a Kindle-compatible format.
- Reading position, notes, and highlights may synchronize.
- A direct USB connection is not required.
- Files remain available for later download.

Manual USB transfers are useful when Internet access is unavailable or when you do not want a document stored in your Amazon library.

<br>

## Supported Send to Kindle File Types

Amazon's Send to Kindle service currently supports the following file types:

- EPUB
- PDF
- DOC
- DOCX
- TXT
- RTF
- HTM
- HTML
- PNG
- GIF
- JPG
- JPEG
- BMP

The browser-based Send to Kindle service supports individual files up to **200 MB**.

File support can change over time, so review the [official Send to Kindle page](https://www.amazon.com/sendtokindle) when transferring an unusual or particularly large document.

<br>

## EPUB Versus PDF

EPUB and PDF files behave differently when displayed on a Kindle.

### EPUB Files

EPUB is a reflowable eBook format.

A reflowable document can adjust its text to match:

- Screen size
- Font size
- Font style
- Line spacing
- Page margins
- Reading orientation

When an EPUB file is submitted through Send to Kindle, Amazon converts it into a Kindle-compatible format.

EPUB is generally the better option for:

- Novels
- Text-heavy books
- Essays
- Long-form reading
- Documents without complex formatting

Do not simply copy an EPUB file to an older Kindle through USB and assume it will work. The more reliable method is to submit the EPUB through Send to Kindle so Amazon can convert it.

### PDF Files

A PDF normally uses a fixed page layout.

This means the page retains its original:

- Dimensions
- Fonts
- Images
- Columns
- Margins
- Page breaks

PDF files are useful for:

- Technical manuals
- Forms
- Research papers
- Scanned documents
- Textbooks
- Documents with diagrams
- Documents requiring exact formatting

However, PDFs designed for full-size paper may be difficult to read on a smaller Kindle screen. Users may need to zoom and move around the page.

A Kindle Scribe or another large-screen Kindle is generally better suited for complex PDFs.

## Recommended Method: Send to Kindle from a Web Browser

The browser-based Send to Kindle service is the easiest method for most users.

To transfer a book or PDF:

- Open the [Amazon Send to Kindle page](https://www.amazon.com/sendtokindle).
- Sign in with the Amazon account registered to your Kindle.
- Select **Select files from device**.
- Browse to the file on your computer.
- Select the book, PDF, or document.
- Confirm that **Add to your library** is enabled.
- Select **Send**.
- Wait for Amazon to process the file.
- Connect your Kindle to Wi-Fi.
- Synchronize the Kindle library.

You can also drag and drop files directly onto the Send to Kindle webpage.

Processing time depends on:

- File size
- File type
- Document complexity
- Internet connection
- Current Amazon service activity

Small files may appear quickly, while large or complex documents may require more time.

<br>

## Why Add to Your Library Matters

The **Add to your library** option determines whether the uploaded document is stored in your Kindle library.

When this option is enabled:

- The document is associated with your Amazon account.
- It can be downloaded again later.
- It may appear on compatible Kindle devices.
- It may appear in compatible Kindle applications.
- Reading progress may synchronize.
- Notes and highlights may synchronize when supported.

When this option is disabled, Amazon may deliver the file only to the selected device.

For most users, leaving **Add to your library** enabled provides the best experience.

<br>

## Find the Document on Your Kindle

After sending the document:

- Connect the Kindle to Wi-Fi.
- Open the Kindle home screen.
- Select **Library**.
- Change the library view to **All** if necessary.
- Open the filter options.
- Select **Documents** if the file is not immediately visible.
- Select the document to download and open it.

If the document does not appear, open the Kindle menu and select:

**Sync Your Kindle**

Depending on the Kindle model and software version, the option may also appear as:

**Sync and Check for Items**

Make sure that the Kindle is registered to the same Amazon account used on the Send to Kindle website.

<br>

## Transfer a File Using Your Kindle Email Address

Every compatible Kindle device and Kindle application can have its own Send to Kindle email address.

The address usually ends with:

`@kindle.com`

To send a document by email:

- Locate the Send to Kindle email address for your device.
- Add your personal email address to Amazon's approved sender list.
- Create a new email from the approved address.
- Attach the supported book or document.
- Send the message to the Kindle email address.
- Connect the Kindle to Wi-Fi.
- Synchronize the device.

The email body and subject can generally remain blank.

Amazon accepts documents only from approved email addresses. This prevents unknown senders from delivering unwanted files to your Kindle library.

<br>

## Find Your Send to Kindle Email Address

To locate the Kindle email address:

- Sign in to your Amazon account.
- Open **Manage Your Content and Devices**.
- Select **Preferences**.
- Expand **Personal Document Settings**.
- Locate **Send to Kindle Email Settings**.
- Find the email address associated with the correct Kindle device or application.

Each registered Kindle may have a different email address.

You can customize the address from the same settings area. Choose an address that is not easy for strangers to guess.

Amazon provides additional instructions in its [Send to Kindle email documentation](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=G7NECT4B4ZWHQ8WV).

<br>

## Add an Approved Personal Document Email Address

Before emailing a document, add the sending address to the approved list.

To add an address:

- Sign in to your Amazon account.
- Open **Manage Your Content and Devices**.
- Select **Preferences**.
- Expand **Personal Document Settings**.
- Locate **Approved Personal Document Email List**.
- Select **Add a new approved email address**.
- Enter the complete email address.
- Save the change.

Amazon requires complete email addresses rather than broad domain-only approvals.

For example, approve:

`reader@example.com`

Do not assume that approving only the email domain will permit every address from that domain.

Additional details are available in Amazon's [approved document email list instructions](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=GX9XLEVV8G4DB28H).

<br>

## Email Limitations

Email is convenient, but it has more limitations than the browser-based Send to Kindle service.

Potential limitations include:

- Email attachment size restrictions
- Restrictions imposed by your email provider
- Approved sender requirements
- Delivery verification requests
- Processing delays
- Rejected unsupported files
- Rejected password-protected documents
- A limit on the number of approved sending addresses

Amazon may request that you verify a document submission through an email sent to the address associated with your Amazon account.

Complete the verification before the deadline included in the message.

For large files, use the browser-based Send to Kindle page instead of email.

## Send Books and PDFs from an iPhone or iPad

The Kindle application can receive supported files through the iOS sharing menu.

First, install the Kindle application and sign in with the Amazon account associated with your Kindle.

To send a file:

- Open the **Files** application or another application containing the document.
- Locate the EPUB, PDF, or supported file.
- Select the **Share** button.
- Select **Kindle** from the application list.
- Enter or review the document title and author.
- Enable **Save in Kindle Library** when available.
- Select **Send**.
- Wait for the upload to complete.
- Synchronize the Kindle device.

If Kindle does not appear in the sharing menu:

- Scroll through the application list.
- Select **More**.
- Enable or select **Kindle**.
- Try sharing the file again.

The exact labels may vary depending on the installed iOS and Kindle application versions.

<br>

## Send Books and PDFs from Android

The Kindle application can also receive documents through the Android sharing menu.

To transfer a file:

- Install the Kindle application.
- Sign in with the Amazon account registered to your Kindle.
- Open a file manager, browser, email, or document application.
- Locate the EPUB, PDF, or supported file.
- Select **Share**.
- Select **Kindle**.
- Review the title and author.
- Enable the option to add the document to your Kindle library.
- Select **Send**.
- Synchronize the Kindle device.

The available sharing options depend on the Android manufacturer, Android version, and application being used.

<br>

## Transfer Files from Windows or macOS

Amazon provides Send to Kindle applications for Windows and macOS.

The desktop application can be useful when you regularly transfer files from the same computer.

Depending on the operating system and application version, you may be able to:

- Drag files into the Send to Kindle application.
- Select files through the application.
- Send files from the operating system's sharing menu.
- Right-click a supported file and select a Send to Kindle option.
- Choose whether the document is added to your Kindle library.
- Select a specific Kindle destination.

To begin, review Amazon's [Send to Kindle documentation](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=G5WYD9SAF7PGXRNA) and install the appropriate application for your operating system.

## Transfer Books and PDFs Through USB

A USB connection can be used to copy supported files directly to a Kindle.

This method is useful when:

- The Kindle is offline.
- Wi-Fi is unavailable.
- The document should not be uploaded to Amazon.
- A large local file must be transferred.
- You are working in a restricted environment.

USB-transferred documents differ from files added through Send to Kindle.

Files copied through USB:

- Are stored only on that Kindle.
- Are not automatically added to your Amazon library.
- Do not automatically appear on other devices.
- May not synchronize reading progress.
- May not synchronize notes and highlights.
- Are permanently removed if deleted without retaining another copy.

Keep a backup of any file copied manually.

<br>

## Transfer Files Through USB on Windows

To transfer a document from Windows:

- Connect the Kindle to the computer using a compatible USB data cable.
- Unlock or wake the Kindle if necessary.
- Open **File Explorer**.
- Select the Kindle device.
- Open the Kindle storage.
- Open the **Documents** folder.
- Copy the supported book or PDF into the folder.
- Wait for the transfer to finish.
- Safely eject the Kindle.
- Disconnect the USB cable.
- Open the Kindle library.

Do not disconnect the Kindle while files are still being copied.

A charging-only cable will provide power but will not transfer data. Try another cable if Windows does not detect the Kindle.

<br>

## Transfer Files Through USB on macOS

USB behavior on macOS depends on the Kindle model.

Older Kindle devices may appear directly in Finder as an external drive.

For an older model:

- Connect the Kindle using a compatible USB data cable.
- Open **Finder**.
- Select the Kindle from the sidebar.
- Open the **Documents** folder.
- Copy the supported file into the folder.
- Wait for the transfer to complete.
- Eject the Kindle.
- Disconnect the cable.

Kindle Scribe and Kindle devices released in 2024 or later use a newer USB file-transfer method on macOS. These devices may require a separate compatible application rather than appearing as a traditional removable drive.

Amazon's current Send to Kindle application for macOS supports USB transfers for these devices.

Review the [Amazon USB file-transfer documentation](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=TCUBEdEkbIhK07ysFu) for model-specific instructions.

<br>

## Kindle Only Charges When Connected

If the Kindle charges but does not appear on the computer, check the following:

- Confirm that the cable supports data transfer.
- Try a different USB cable.
- Try another USB port.
- Avoid an unpowered USB hub.
- Restart the Kindle.
- Restart the computer.
- Unlock the Kindle before reconnecting it.
- Install pending Kindle software updates.
- Test the Kindle on another computer.
- Confirm whether the Kindle model requires a dedicated macOS transfer application.

Many USB cables are designed only for charging and cannot transfer files.

The cable is one of the first items to replace when troubleshooting this problem.

## Use Calibre to Manage Personal eBooks

Calibre is a free and open-source eBook management application.

It can help users:

- Organize an eBook library.
- Edit book metadata.
- Add book covers.
- Convert DRM-free files.
- Transfer compatible books through USB.
- Review file formats.
- Manage large personal collections.

Calibre can convert DRM-free files into formats appropriate for different e-readers.

However, conversion is not always necessary. For an EPUB file, using Amazon's Send to Kindle service is often easier because Amazon performs the required conversion.

Calibre should not be used to bypass digital rights management or remove protections from books without authorization.

Download it from the [official Calibre website](https://calibre-ebook.com/).

<br>

## Format Considerations for USB Transfers

Send to Kindle and direct USB transfer are not identical.

Send to Kindle accepts EPUB files because Amazon processes and converts them before delivery.

A direct USB transfer does not provide the same cloud-based conversion process.

For USB transfers, results depend on:

- Kindle model
- Kindle software version
- File format
- File structure
- Digital rights management
- Destination folder
- Whether the file was previously converted

PDF files can normally be copied directly because Kindle devices include PDF support.

For EPUB books, Send to Kindle is the safer and more reliable approach.

## Transfer an Amazon-Purchased Book to a Kindle

Books purchased directly from Amazon do not normally need to be manually uploaded.

To deliver a purchased book:

- Sign in to the Amazon account used to purchase it.
- Open **Manage Your Content and Devices**.
- Select **Content**.
- Locate the book.
- Open the available actions.
- Select **Deliver or Remove from Device**.
- Choose the Kindle.
- Confirm the delivery.
- Connect the Kindle to Wi-Fi.
- Synchronize the device.

You can also open the Kindle library and select the book cover to download it.

Amazon removed the older website option for downloading purchased Kindle books and transferring them through USB in February 2025. Purchased books should now normally be delivered through the Kindle library to a Wi-Fi-connected device.

This does not prevent you from manually transferring your own supported and legally obtained documents through USB.

<br>

## Documents Versus Books in the Kindle Library

Personal files may be categorized as documents rather than books.

If a transferred item is missing:

- Open **Library**.
- Select **All** instead of **Downloaded**.
- Open the filter menu.
- Select **Documents**.
- Clear any active reading or collection filters.
- Search for the title.
- Search for the author.
- Synchronize the Kindle.

A file can be present in the account but hidden by the current library filter.

<br>

## Improve the Title and Author Information

Some personal documents appear with an incorrect title, filename, or author.

When using the mobile or desktop Send to Kindle workflow, review the title and author fields before submitting the file.

For EPUB files, metadata can also be edited before transfer using an eBook management application such as Calibre.

Good metadata makes it easier to:

- Search for the book.
- Sort the library.
- Identify the author.
- Group related documents.
- Distinguish different editions.

PDF metadata may be more difficult to change, and some PDFs may appear using the original filename.

<br>

## Transfer Scanned PDFs

A scanned PDF may contain page images instead of selectable text.

These files can usually be transferred, but they may have limitations:

- Text size cannot reflow.
- Font settings may not work.
- Dictionary lookup may not work.
- Search may not work.
- Highlighting may be limited.
- Pages may load slowly.
- File sizes may be large.
- Small text may be difficult to read.

Optical character recognition can convert scanned page images into searchable text.

Before transferring a scanned document, consider processing it with a trusted OCR application.

Always retain the original file in case the conversion changes the page layout or introduces recognition errors.

<br>

## Transfer Password-Protected PDFs

Password-protected or encrypted PDFs may fail during Send to Kindle processing.

Before uploading the file:

- Open it using an authorized PDF application.
- Confirm that you know the password.
- Remove the protection only when you are permitted to do so.
- Save an unprotected copy.
- Submit the new copy through Send to Kindle.

Do not attempt to remove protections from documents you do not own or have permission to modify.

<br>

## Reading PDFs on a Small Kindle Screen

PDF pages are commonly designed for Letter or A4 paper, which can make them difficult to read on a smaller Kindle.

To improve readability:

- Rotate the Kindle to landscape orientation.
- Reduce page margins before transfer.
- Crop unnecessary white space.
- Use a larger-screen Kindle.
- Convert a text-heavy document into EPUB.
- Use a PDF optimized for smaller screens.
- Use pinch-to-zoom on supported models.
- Divide a large document into smaller sections.

Conversion works best for text-heavy PDFs with simple layouts.

PDFs containing columns, tables, diagrams, formulas, or complex formatting may not convert cleanly.

<br>

## Files That Cannot Be Transferred

A file may fail when it contains:

- Unsupported formatting
- Digital rights management
- Password protection
- Corrupted data
- Invalid EPUB structure
- Extremely complex HTML
- Oversized images
- Unsupported fonts
- A file size beyond the transfer method's limit
- An incorrect file extension

A book purchased from another eBook platform may include DRM that restricts it to that vendor's applications and devices.

Changing the filename or extension does not remove DRM or convert the underlying format.

<br>

## Troubleshoot Send to Kindle Failures

When Send to Kindle fails:

- Confirm that the file type is supported.
- Confirm that the file is no larger than the stated limit.
- Open the file locally to verify that it is not corrupted.
- Remove password protection when authorized.
- Confirm that the EPUB passes basic validation.
- Rename the file using standard letters and numbers.
- Try the browser-based Send to Kindle service.
- Try sending one file at a time.
- Check your email for an Amazon error message.
- Check whether Amazon requested submission verification.
- Confirm that you signed in to the correct Amazon account.

Amazon provides error-specific guidance in its [Send to Kindle troubleshooting documentation](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=T48rsVm3gY7KeGkKUk).

<br>

## Troubleshoot Email Delivery

When an emailed document does not appear:

- Confirm the destination Kindle email address.
- Confirm the sender is on the approved email list.
- Use the complete approved email address.
- Confirm the attachment uses a supported format.
- Check the attachment size.
- Check your inbox for an Amazon verification message.
- Check your inbox for a rejection message.
- Confirm that the Kindle is online.
- Synchronize the Kindle.
- Confirm that both devices use the same Amazon account.

Do not place the Kindle email address in a public location. Although Amazon's approved sender controls provide protection, keeping the address private reduces unwanted delivery attempts.

<br>

## Troubleshoot Missing Library Items

When Amazon accepts the document but it does not appear:

- Connect the Kindle to Wi-Fi.
- Select **Sync Your Kindle**.
- Change the library view from **Downloaded** to **All**.
- Clear active filters.
- Select the **Documents** filter.
- Search for the filename.
- Search for the author.
- Restart the Kindle.
- Verify that the Kindle is registered to the correct Amazon account.
- Review the personal documents in **Manage Your Content and Devices**.

A document uploaded without **Add to your library** enabled may have been delivered only to a selected device.

<br>

## Restart the Kindle

A restart can resolve synchronization, indexing, and library display problems.

To restart most Kindle devices:

- Open **Settings**.
- Select **Device Options**.
- Select **Restart**.

You can also press and hold the power button until the power menu appears, then select **Restart**.

Do not select a factory reset unless you understand that it removes downloaded content and account configuration from the device.

<br>

## Keep a Backup of Personal Documents

Do not rely on the Kindle as the only storage location for an important document.

Maintain another copy in a secure location such as:

- A computer
- An external drive
- A network storage system
- An encrypted cloud storage service
- A documented backup system

This is particularly important for USB-transferred documents because they are not automatically archived in the Kindle library.

A factory reset, hardware failure, accidental deletion, or lost device could permanently remove a file stored only on the Kindle.

<br>

## Privacy Considerations

Files submitted through Send to Kindle are processed through Amazon's service and may be stored in your Kindle library.

For sensitive documents, consider:

- Whether the document is appropriate for cloud processing
- Whether organizational policies permit the upload
- Whether the document contains personal information
- Whether the file should remain only on local devices
- Whether USB transfer is a better option
- Whether the Kindle has a device passcode
- Whether the Amazon account uses multifactor authentication

Avoid transferring restricted business, medical, financial, legal, or government documents unless the transfer method complies with the applicable policies and security requirements.

## Recommended Method by Situation

Use the browser-based Send to Kindle service when:

- You have an EPUB book.
- You want Amazon to convert the file.
- You want the document available across devices.
- You want the file stored in your Kindle library.
- The file is too large for email.

Use the Kindle email address when:

- You need a fast email-based workflow.
- The file is already attached to an email.
- The sending address is approved.
- The file is relatively small.

Use mobile sharing when:

- The file is on an iPhone, iPad, or Android device.
- You downloaded the file from a browser.
- You received the file through email.
- You want to send it without using a computer.

Use USB transfer when:

- The Kindle cannot connect to Wi-Fi.
- You do not want to upload the file to Amazon.
- The document is already in a directly supported format.
- You understand that the file will remain local to that Kindle.

<br>

## Conclusion

Amazon provides several practical methods for transferring personal books and PDFs to a Kindle. The browser-based Send to Kindle service is the best option for most users because it accepts common formats, converts EPUB files, stores documents in the Kindle library, and makes them available across compatible devices.

Email and mobile sharing provide convenient alternatives, while USB transfer is useful for offline or privacy-sensitive workflows. However, documents copied through USB remain local to the device and do not receive the synchronization benefits provided by the Kindle library.

For the most reliable experience, use Send to Kindle for EPUB books, verify that personal documents are added to your library, keep the Kindle connected to Wi-Fi, and retain a separate backup of every important file.
